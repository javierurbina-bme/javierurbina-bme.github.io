## 1. High-Resolution Tractography of Superficial White Matter Across the Lifespan  
**MRI · Diffusion Imaging · Tractography · University of Alberta**

This project investigates lifespan-related maturation and aging patterns of superficial white matter (SWM) using high-resolution diffusion MRI (1.5 mm isotropic) and multi-shell constrained spherical deconvolution (CSD).  
The study included **185 participants aged 5–74 years**, enabling the first SWM tractography analysis across the full human lifespan.

Key contributions include:
- Advanced preprocessing (NLSAM denoising, eddy correction)  
- Multi-shell CSD tractography with ACT and GM–GM filtering  
- Novel SWM seeding mask generation directly in diffusion space  
- Lifespan FA/MD curves showing delayed maturation of SWM relative to deep white matter  

This work was presented at the **International Society of Tractography Conference (2025)** and **ISMRM (2026)**.

## 2. Music-Based Video Game Therapy for Hand Rehabilitation in Children with Cerebral Palsy  
**Neurorehabilitation · Wearable Sensors · Unity · PUCP**

This project integrates **Neurologic Music Therapy** principles with **video game–based rehabilitation** to improve wrist and hand motor function in children with cerebral palsy.

System features:
- Three therapeutic games (Bubble, Rocket, Squirrel)  
- Real-time IMU-based motion tracking (BNO055 sensors)  
- Music-driven cues (metronome timing, volume feedback)  
- Therapist configuration interface (movement type, difficulty, repetitions)

Pilot study (n=10 healthy children, 8–12 years):
- Music reduced movement variability across tasks  
- Improved consistency and smoother trajectories  
- Positive user experience (relaxing, engaging)

Published in **Journal of NeuroEngineering and Rehabilitation (2025)**.

## 3. Nonlinear Ultrasound Parameter Estimation Using the Extended Comparative Method (ECM)  
**Ultrasound · K-Wave Simulation · Tissue Characterization · PUCP**

This project estimates the nonlinear acoustic parameter **B/A** using an adaptation of the Extended Comparative Method (ECM), leveraging second harmonic ultrasound propagation.

Technical contributions:
- K-Wave simulations of nonlinear media  
- FIR bandpass filtering centered on the second harmonic  
- Calculation of nonlinear coefficient maps  
- Total Variation (TV) filtering to preserve structural boundaries  
- Comparison between homogeneous and inhomogeneous media

Applications:
- Tissue characterization (e.g., fatty liver disease)  
- Harmonic imaging enhancement  
- Nonlinear propagation modeling
