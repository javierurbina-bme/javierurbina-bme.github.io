I am Javier Urbina Alarcón, a Biomedical Engineering and Medical Physics M.Sc. student at the Technical University of Munich (TUM). My academic and professional path spans medical imaging, biomedical signal processing, medical device development, and translational research aimed at improving clinical diagnostics and patient care.

I currently work at the Klinikum rechts der Isar (TUM) in Diagnostic and Interventional Neuroradiology, where I support clinical workflows and gain hands-on experience with MRI, angiography, and advanced imaging procedures. This role has strengthened my understanding of clinical environments, imaging protocols, and multidisciplinary collaboration.

My research background includes high-resolution diffusion MRI at the Peter S. Allen MRI Research Centre (University of Alberta), where I investigated lifespan-related changes in superficial white matter using advanced tractography methods. I have also worked on ultrasound simulation and tissue characterization, ECG signal processing for medical device calibration, and the development of wearable sensor-based neurorehabilitation systems for children with cerebral palsy.

I have contributed to peer-reviewed publications and international conference presentations in neuroimaging, neurorehabilitation, and medical device engineering. My work has been supported by competitive scholarships and research grants, including the Emerging Leaders in the Americas Program (ELAP) funded by Global Affairs Canada.

My goal is to contribute to the medical technology industry by developing imaging tools, devices, and computational methods that support clinicians and improve patient outcomes.
